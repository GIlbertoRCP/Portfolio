      <h2 class="text-2xl font-display font-extrabold text-white mb-3">Project Concept</h2>
      <p>
        Formula 1 performance analysis is heavily distorted by race parameters like tyre compound choices, degradation curves, and varying fuel levels. The <strong>F1 Oracle</strong> scrapes micro-telemetry streams from official F1 sessions, applies physical correction equations to detrend raw times, and feeds the features to a pairwise ranking model that determines probability vectors for finishing positions.
      </p>

    <div>
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-display font-extrabold text-white mb-3">Engineered Telemetry Features</h2>
      <p>
        Standard practice focuses solely on raw lap times. This project engineered customized columns from micro-telemetry streams:
      </p>
      
      <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2 mt-2">
        <li><strong>Fuel-Corrected Pace:</strong> Subtracts estimated lap fuel burning weights (adjusted dynamically for tracks, e.g. Monaco vs Spa) and applies a penalty coefficient (~0.03s per kg) to find a driver's true pace.</li>
        <li><strong>Tyre Degradation Rate:</strong> Computes the linear regression slope of lap times vs. tyre age across stints of at least 5 clean laps.</li>
        <li><strong>ERS Efficiency Proxy:</strong> Calculates the ratio of full-throttle time to braking recovery time during a driver's best push lap relative to the field median.</li>
        <li><strong>Asymmetric PU Deployment:</strong> Sector 1/Sector 3 ratios to detect battery starvation and ERS clipping.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-display font-extrabold text-white mb-3">Machine Learning Modeling</h2>
      <p>
        We train an <code>XGBRanker</code> gradient boosted tree using a <code>rank:pairwise</code> loss function. Instead of predicting absolute times, the ranker learns driver ordering constraints per race.
      </p>
      <p>
        The training data utilizes a temporal decay function (weight = exp(-λ * Δt)) where recent 2026 races are heavily weighted, and historical data (2024-2025) provides weaker priors, accounting for structural regulation shifts.
      </p>
    </div>
