    {/* Section 1: The Problem */}
    <div class="space-y-4">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-display font-extrabold text-white flex items-center gap-2">
        <span class="text-purple-500 text-xl font-mono">01/</span> The F1 Telemetry Challenge
      </h2>
      <p>
        Evaluating true driver pace in Formula 1 is notoriously difficult. A driver's raw lap time is distorted by track rubbering-in, tyre age, engine modes, and fuel weight load. Additionally, standard regression models that predict absolute lap times fail in racing applications because finishing order constraints matter far more than time intervals.
      </p>
      <p>
        To solve this, the **F1 Oracle** implements physical telemetry correction formulas to filter out distortions, then uses a pairwise preference ranking model to predict the final order and simulate probability curves.
      </p>
    </div>

      <h2 class="text-2xl font-display font-extrabold text-white flex items-center gap-2">
        <span class="text-purple-500 text-xl font-mono">03/</span> Physics-Based Feature Engineering
      </h2>
      <p>
        Before training the model, we filter out laps under safety car or pit lane exits and extract micro-telemetry streams. We then compute customized features:
      </p>
      
      <div class="grid sm:grid-cols-2 gap-6 pt-4">
        <div class="bg-slate-950 border border-slate-900 rounded-xl p-5 space-y-2 hover:border-slate-800 transition">
          <div class="text-purple-400 font-bold text-sm">⏱️ Fuel-Corrected Pace</div>
          <p class="text-[11px] text-slate-500 leading-relaxed">
            Subtracts estimated lap fuel burning weights (adjusted dynamically for tracks, e.g. Monaco vs Spa) and applies a penalty coefficient (~0.03s per kg) to find a driver's true pace.
          </p>
        </div>
        
        <div class="bg-slate-950 border border-slate-900 rounded-xl p-5 space-y-2 hover:border-slate-800 transition">
          <div class="text-purple-400 font-bold text-sm">📈 Tyre Degradation Rate</div>
          <p class="text-[11px] text-slate-500 leading-relaxed">
            Computes the linear regression slope of lap times vs. tyre age across stints of at least 5 clean laps.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="bg-slate-950 border border-slate-900 rounded-xl p-5 space-y-2 hover:border-slate-800 transition">
          <div class="text-purple-400 font-bold text-sm">⚡ ERS Efficiency Proxy</div>
          <p class="text-[11px] text-slate-500 leading-relaxed">
            Calculates the ratio of full-throttle time to braking recovery time during a driver's best push lap relative to the field median.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="bg-slate-950 border border-slate-900 rounded-xl p-5 space-y-2 hover:border-slate-800 transition">
          <div class="text-purple-400 font-bold text-sm">🔋 Asymmetric PU Deployment</div>
          <p class="text-[11px] text-slate-500 leading-relaxed">
            Calculates Sector 1/Sector 3 ratios to detect battery starvation and ERS clipping.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    {/* Section 4: Pairwise XGBoost Model */}
    <div class="space-y-4">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-display font-extrabold text-white flex items-center gap-2">
        <span class="text-purple-500 text-xl font-mono">04/</span> Pairwise XGBRanker &amp; Decay
      </h2>
      <p>
        The prediction engine trains an <code>XGBRanker</code> gradient boosted tree using a <code>rank:pairwise</code> loss function. This treats each race as a ranking group and learns relative preferences between drivers.
      </p>
      <p>
        The training data utilizes a temporal decay function (weight = exp(-λ * Δt)), where recent 2026 races are heavily weighted, and historical data provides weaker priors, accounting for structural regulation shifts.
      </p>
    </div>

    {/* Section 5: Prefetching Pipeline */}
    <div class="space-y-4">

      </h2>
      <p>
        F1 telemetry payloads are extremely heavy. To prevent long wait times on a cold cache, the FastAPI backend implements background prefetching using <code>BackgroundTasks</code>:
      </p>
